Chilean, applied mathematician, I've spent the last seven years in France as graduate student (masters, Phd) at Université Paris-Dauphine, then as postdoctoral fellow at Géoazur CNRS-Université Côte d'Azur. Struggling with french bureaucracy all the time.

My research focuses on the development and analysis of algorithms for solving differential equations through Domain Decomposition Methods (Parareal, Schwarz), with possible applications to optimization and data assimilation problems. I'm curious about scientific computing.

In a more personal note, among my hobbies are photography, biking, reading and traveling. I'm quite interested in politics.
